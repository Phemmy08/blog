Prophet Abraham Adebayo

Prophet Founder & Set Man · Grace for All Men International Ministry

Biography

Prophet Abraham Adebayo is the founder and Set Man of the Grace for All Men International Ministry (GFAM), a non-denominational ministry with the mandate to raise men in purity, power, prosperity, and consecration — popularly known as the Cave of Adullam, a place where generals are made. He hails from Erin-Ile in Kwara State and was born into the Cherubim & Seraphim fold.

His journey to faith began in April 1997 during a crusade of Blessed Ministry of C&S Church Oke-Igbala, Erin-Ile, under the ministration of Evangelist Mike Fatoki (now Jesutoki). The encounter was so profound that he did not wait for the altar call — he ran forward before it was given. From that moment, his life in Christ began.

The call to full-time ministry came in 2008. Though he had plans to earn a PhD in History and International Relations and pursue only part-time ministry, God spoke clearly while he was with C&S King's Court Chapel, Victoria Island, Lagos. After six months of daily prayer and vigils, and three months of fasting, he surrendered fully. On 31st May 2008 he left Lagos, and on 1st June 2008 he began the work in Erin-Ile as a lone soldier — carrying God's word: "I cause my words in your mouth from the corner to shake the center."

Prophet Abraham is the host of the annual Path of the Father's Conference, held in Erin-Ile, Kwara State, and also convenes the 72 Hours Intercession for Cherubim and Seraphim every year as directed by God. He is widely recognised as one of the trailblazing C&S pastors redefining the scope of the denomination towards what Christ truly intended it to be, with a strong emphasis on purity, precision, and accuracy in the prophetic ministry.

"I cause my words in your mouth from the corner to shake the center."

— God's Word to Prophet Abraham Adebayo

Prophet Abraham is happily married to his heartthrob, Rachael Abraham. They were joined in holy matrimony on 10th July 2015, and together they carry on the banner of our Lord Jesus Christ.
